Day countc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/DayCount

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/
An Act of Parliament may define a day count during which Parliament may scrutinise an item of secondary legislation. These may be sitting days or statutory days, as described in the time period ontology. The Statutory Instruments Act 1946 defines a default period of 40 statutory days of scrutiny for instruments subject to parliamentary procedure. The enabling Act may define an exception to this default - commonly 28, 60 or 120 days. The European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018 defines a period of 10 sitting days of scrutiny for proposed negative statutory instruments.

Procedurec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/Procedure

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/
A procedure in a House or Houses. For example, the passage of a made affirmative statutory instrument in both Houses. A procedure may consist of business in a particular House, such as a division; business undertaken by both Houses jointly, such as a joint committee; processes applying to either House, such as a time period; and activities outside Parliament, such as the making of a statutory instrument. In this model a procedure that consists of business in both Houses is considered as a single procedure.

Statutory instrument series itemc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/StatutoryInstrumentSeriesItem

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/
A statutory instrument series item links a draft or made statutory instrument subject to parliamentary procedure to a statutory instrument series. A statutory instrument can be included by means of an item in more than one series excepting these cases: Northern Ireland series instruments do not form part of any other series; legal series instruments do not form part of any other series; National Assembly for Wales series SIs and Scottish series instruments can be included in the commencement order series but no other. Commencement order series items may also form part of either the Welsh or Scottish series. Where an instrument appears as an item in more than one series, the citation order is formed by (1) the commencement order series and item identifier followed by (2) any other series and item identifier. Where the instrument is made by the Welsh Ministers, the 'W. / Cy.' number follows the SI number.
is in domain of
In seriesop, SI series item identifierdp
is in range of
As series itemop

Imposes day count dutyop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/imposesDayCountDuty

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/
Relates an enabling power to a day count. For statutory instruments the default count is 40 statutory days, as set out by the Statutory Instruments Act 1946. An enabling Act may override this count. A day count is set by a single Act of Parliament. A single Act of Parliament may specify differing day counts for different enabling powers. A statutory instrument is required to have a single day count when enabling powers are delegated from more than one Act of Parliament.
